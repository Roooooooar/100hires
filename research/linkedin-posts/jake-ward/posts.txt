POST 1:

SEO is not a one-person job.

It'll only be a successful marketing channel if the resources are available to execute it properly.

So if you're hiring one person to do:

- Strategy
- AI search
- Digital PR
- Link building
- Data analysis
- Topic research
- Content creation
- Technical and coding

That's an SEO team.

Not a single hire.

PS. A lot of "SEO isn't working" problems are actually resourcing problems.

POST 2:

Planning SEO content for the 2nd half of 2026?

PLEASE do this first:

1. Get all your blog URLs into a spreadsheet.

Use Screaming Frog to crawl your site and export the URLs. Then enrich the sheet with data from:
- Ahrefs
- Google Analytics
- Google Search Console

2. Find the keywords each important URL ranks for.

Use Google Search Console or Ahrefs, then add:
- Primary keyword
- Secondary keywords / variations
- Current rankings for target keywords

3. Audit every post and assign an action:
- Leave: already reached its potential
- Merge: cannibalising or too similar to another page
- Delete: no longer useful or valuable
- Update: small improvements needed
- Rewrite: major improvements needed

4. Prioritise updates and rewrites where:
- target keywords sit in positions 4-20
- the page has higher conversion or revenue potential
- there are obvious improvement opportunities
- the page isn't yet being retrieved by LLMs

POST 4:

43.8% of AI citations come from "best X" lists.

That's more than blog posts, landing pages, product pages, and homepages combined.

Here's the playbook:

1. Earn third-party recommendations
2. Get on the lists AI already trusts
3. Aim for the top, not just inclusion
4. Create your own list pages
5. Keep these pages fresh
6. Don't let domain authority stop you
7. Build consensus across the web
8. Track where competitors are being recommended
9. Think beyond rankings

POST 5:

GEO, AEO, AIO, LLMO… LMAO.

Every month there's a new acronym, a new "discipline," a new agency charging a premium to do… mostly SEO.

Here's the honest take nobody selling AEO services wants you to hear:

1. 90% of "AEO" is just SEO done well
2. llms.txt files have zero measurable impact
3. .md pages aren't being fetched by anyone
4. Schema markup isn't a magic AEO lever

The 10% that actually matters?
Earned mentions + ranking in Google.
