POST 1:

Breaking: Cloudflare and beehiiv just announced AI Crawl Controls for creators and I genuinely don't know how to feel about this.

On one hand, I've built my entire career on discovery.

Getting SaaS brands found.

Making sure the right people see their work in the right places at the right time.

These platforms are how brands get bought.

On the other hand, I get it.

For a lot of writers, publishers and creators, your archive is valuable.

If AI companies want to train on your original thinking, maybe you should get paid.

But here's where I get stuck:

The payouts.

Cloudflare talks about a Spotify model for creators.

But music and writing aren't the same thing.

Noah Kahan gets 31M monthly listeners. I've listened to his new album (at least) 50 times in between Bad Bunny, yacht rock and my 2006 warped tour playlist.

(don't judge)

But an article gets scraped once and summarized forever.

The economics don't translate cleanly, especially when we're hoping from payouts from companies that lose 21B per year.

And yet....

OpenAI doesn't want the 27th copycat blog about what happened in politics today. Their value to users is surfacing original thinking. That's where creators actually have leverage.

So maybe this is less about blocking AI crawlers today and more about signaling that your content has future licensing value.

I'm not sure if the payouts will ever be worth it, especially for tech companies.

Or if every website should have push-button controls to block ai crawlers.

But I'm watching this closely.

POST 4:

After 100s of hours with AI, I'm realizing Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity... they're all worthless until you do these 4 things:

You know the ability to pick between three headlines and know which one will land?

How to sense when your marketing is technically correct but nobody's going to care?

It's called TASTE.

Good news: taste is something you can learn (and get good at).

Sam Parr described it recently and I put it into a framework so I can remember it.

I call it the 4D framework and it's honestly the answer to the whole "AI is coming for your job" panic.

1. Decide what you actually want to say.
2. Duplicate the people already saying it well. Blindly. Shamelessly.
3. Decode the rules underneath why it works.
4. Defy the rules

Whether you’re studying Apple or Dr. Dre, it’s the same pattern.

People decided what they wanted to say, studied what was already out there, figured out why it worked and then decided what rules to break.

AI can match patterns. It can't decide which pattern to break.

That's where YOU live, my friend. That's where the value lives.

If you're spending all your time building workflows to remove yourself from the process, you might be automating the wrong part.

And when everyone's letting AI do the thinking, we’re gonna be the ones making stuff worth caring about.

PS - I could automate my client "3S" interviews. I like doing them. I catch things. Ideas hit me mid-conversation. Not everything that can be automated should be.
