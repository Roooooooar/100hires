# Research Notes

## Approach

Started by identifying practitioners — people actively running AI SEO campaigns, building tools, or documenting real experiments — not just commentators writing about the topic.

Avoided generic SEO influencers who post tips without evidence of real client work or experiments.

## LinkedIn Curation Logic

For each LinkedIn expert, selected posts that were specifically about:
- AI content production workflows
- LLM citation and AEO strategies
- Real experiments with documented outcomes
- Content scaling systems and tooling

Avoided milestone posts, personal updates, and generic SEO opinion posts.

## YouTube Collection

Used YouTube Data API v3 to pull the 5 most recent videos per channel. Transcripts fetched using youtube-transcript-api. Script saved in /research/fetch_youtube.py.

## Observations So Far

- Jake Ward and Ryan Law are the most data-driven — both post original research with numbers
- Daniel Foley Carter is the most technically hands-on — actually building and testing AI SEO agents
- Ryan Law's llms.txt research (97% got zero requests) is a strong counter-narrative to the hype
- Matt Diggity's recent shift toward AI citation content shows even affiliate SEOs are pivoting
- Vasco and Edward Sturm are the most product-led — both building tools on top of their content
